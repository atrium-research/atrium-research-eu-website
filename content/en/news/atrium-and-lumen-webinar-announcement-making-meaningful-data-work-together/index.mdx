Webinar: Making meaningful data work together: *Exploring interoperability, semantic artefacts, and scalable solutions with ATRIUM and LUMEN*

What does it take for research data to travel across disciplines, languages, and borders? A webinar on **12 May at 11 CEST** explored how interoperability and semantic artefacts, such as shared vocabularies, ontologies, and metadata schemas, can transform the way research data is shared, reused, and scaled across Arts, Social Sciences, and Humanities. 

The session unpacked why interoperability matters and clarified the role and value of semantic artefacts. Drawing on the FAIR principles, the speakers examined key challenges in data integration and presented concrete solutions to address them. They also emphasised the growing need to industrialise the creation and management of semantic artefacts to make research infrastructures more efficient, sustainable, and interoperable at scale.

This online session, organised jointly by ATRIUM and LUMEN EU-funded projects, was aimed at project communities and researchers working with semantic artefacts: including ontologies, controlled vocabularies, and metadata schemas.

Speakers: 

* Massimiliano Carloni (OEAW / ATRIUM)
* Julien Homo (Foxcub / ATRIUM & LUMEN)
* Florian Lemoine (e-Science Data Factory / LUMEN)

[ATRIUM](https://atrium-research.eu/) aims to exploit and strengthen complementarities between leading European infrastructures – DARIAH, ARIADNE, CLARIN, and OPERAS – in order to provide vastly improved access to state-of-the-art services available to researchers across countries, languages, domains and media. 

The [LUMEN project](https://lumenproject.eu/) aims to advance cross-domain collaboration and discovery processes in four academic fields: Mathematics (Maths), Social Sciences and Humanities (SSH), Earth System (ES) and Molecular Dynamics (MD). Through interdisciplinary solutions spanning all four domains, LUMEN sets out to significantly improve discovery by innovation, simplifying initial research phases and facilitating access to advanced AI-powered tools for researchers.
